In geometry, the words \textit{point, line,} and \textit{plane} are \textbf{undefined terms}.
These words do not have formal definitions, but there is agreement about
what they mean.

\begin{keyconceptbox}
  \keyconcepttitle{Undefined Terms}

  \textbf{Point}\quad A \textbf{point} has no dimension. It is represented by a dot.

  \begin{center}
    \includegraphics[width=0.2\linewidth]{figures/chapter-1/point.pdf}
    \captionof*{figure}{Point $A$}
    \label{fig:point}
  \end{center}

  \textbf{Line}\quad A \textbf{line} has one dimension. It is
  represented by a line with two arrowheads, but it extends without end.

  Through any two points, there is exactly one line. You can use any two points on a line
  to name it.

\end{keyconceptbox}
